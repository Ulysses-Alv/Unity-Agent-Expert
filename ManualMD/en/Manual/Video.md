# Video and cutscenes

Use Unity’s video system to integrate video into your application. Video footage can add realism to your game, reduce rendering complexity, or help you integrate content available externally. Unity’s video features include the [Hardware-accelerated and software-based decoding](VideoSources-VideoFiles.md) of video sources, [Transparency support](VideoTransparency.md), multiple [Audio sources](class-AudioSource.md), and [Network streaming](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.renderstreaming@3.1/manual/video-streaming.html).

To use video in Unity, [Import your video sources](video-clips-use.md) and configure them using the [Video Player component](class-VideoPlayer.md).

**Note:** If you don’t want to use externally pre-rendered video, use the [Timeline package](https://docs.unity3d.com/Packages/com.unity.timeline@latest?/manual/index.html) to create cutscenes and cinematic sequences in Unity.
